Project

Most interactions between cultures require the overcoming a language barrier, which is why multilingual speakers play an important role in facilitating such interactions. In addition, certain languages–not necessarily the most spoken ones–are more likely than others to serve as intermediary languages.

We present the Global Language Network (GLN), a new approach for studying global networks using data generated by tens of millions of speakers from all over the world. The GLN was created from the following sources:

Twitter
550 million tweets by over 17 million unique users
Wikipedia
382 million Wikipedia edits by 2.5 million editors in 238 Wikipedia language editions
Books
2.2 million translations of printed books in over a thousand languages, as collected by UNESCO's Index Translationum

The result is an interactive network that shows how language groups influence each other. Looking at the GLN, one can easily see which languages are the most connected and thus most influential, and explore potential paths through which information transfers from one group to another.

Applications of the GLN include promotion of cultural interactions, prediction of trends, and marketing. In this demo, you are welcome to check out the global popularity of your company by language groups. You will find which language group is aware your brand, how its fame spread along the years, and its potential reach. When you're done checking how you fare against the competition, learn how Nobel laureates become famous around the globe, literally minutes after the announcement.
